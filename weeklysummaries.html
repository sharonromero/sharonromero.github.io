Weekly Summary November 5-9

I was excited to visit Fuse Makerspace. I had read about it prior to joining the bootcamp and wanted to see what it was like. It was interesting to hear about all that goes on there and the projects that people are working on. It is a great public resource.

1 Million Cups was interesting. I didn't know a place like that existed and it is nice to know that entrepreneurs can go there for help. I always like finding out about various resources that are available to the public. I will definitely keep it in mind should I ever become an entrepreneur in the future or if I ever come across an entrepreneur who can use the resources from 1 Million Cups.

When we were told that we would be going to 1 Million Cups, I wasn't excited about having to talk to people I don't know and have never met. Expecting that of me and many of the other people in the class was a little bit annoying because if anyone knows anything about introverts, it's that we hate small talk. Small talk is actually a huge pet peeve of mine and asking an introvert to go out and make small talk with strangers is quite annoying. I wish extroverts would take some time to understand introverts instead of just focuing on themselves. Extroverts seem to think that by forcing introverts to go out and meet new people and engage in small talk with a complete stranger will magically turn us into extroverts if we do it enough. That is totally untrue, though. Keeping to ourselves is a part of our personality and hard-wired into our DNA. It is something that can't be changed. Asking introverts to go out and talk with people we don't know is like asking an extrovert not to talk for an entire day or to lock themselves in a room for an entire day with no human interaction. I'm sure the extrovert would be completely miserable.

I didn't talk to anyone attending 1 Million cups but I did talk to some fellow Deep Dive coding bootcamp students that I have never talked to or really talked to before. I had the chance to talk with one of my own classmates, which was nice since I didn't know much about him.

I also had the chance to meet and talk with one of the Java students. I also heard that there were too many Deep Dive coding bootcamp students visiting 1 Million Cups. It turned out that there were too many Deep Dive Coding Bootcamp students attending the meeting because they had already talked to bootcamp student and didn't want to talk to another one.

I didn't interview a Deep Dive Coding Bootcamp alumni because I don't even know where to find one.
